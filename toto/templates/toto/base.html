Understanding About Blood and Donation

What is Blood?

Blood is a fluid that carries oxygen, nutrients, and waste throughout the body. It consists of red blood cells, white blood cells, platelets, and plasma—each playing a vital role in health and immunity.

What is Blood Donation?

Blood donation is the process of voluntarily giving your blood so it can be used for transfusions, emergencies, surgeries, and people with medical conditions. It’s safe, simple, and life-saving.

Why is Blood Donation Important?

Every two seconds, someone needs blood. Hospitals, cancer patients, accident victims, and surgeries rely on blood donors to survive. Since blood cannot be manufactured, your donation is critical.

How Blood Donation Helps People

Blood transfusions save lives. From trauma patients to those with chronic diseases, your single donation can help up to three people. It gives them a second chance at life, health, and happiness.
